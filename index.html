VAPAR · Mpumalanga, South Africa

Learning together for stronger and more equitable health systems

VAPAR brings communities, health workers, government and researchers together to understand local health priorities, act on evidence and learn through implementation.

Based in Mpumalanga, South Africa, the platform combines verbal autopsy, participatory action research and long-term health systems partnerships. Since 2015, it has supported communities and frontline health workers to make local knowledge visible, strengthen dialogue with decision-makers and develop practical responses to persistent health and service challenges.

Featured · Provincial scale-up 2024–26

Enhancing Community Health Worker roles across Mpumalanga

In 2024, VAPAR and Mpumalanga Department of Health delivered participatory community mobilisation training across all three provincial health districts.

Community Health Workers and Outreach Team Leaders used practical participatory tools to analyse service challenges, engage communities and colleagues, plan collective action and learn through implementation.

The subsequent evaluation found rapid peer-to-peer sharing of the tools beyond the original trainees, alongside early steps towards integration within Health Promotion and provincial training systems.

The learning platform

Connecting community knowledge, health systems and action

VAPAR develops relationships and practical processes through which service users, frontline workers, managers and researchers can produce and interpret evidence together.

The platform combines mortality evidence with lived experience, participatory analysis and collective action. This helps partners understand how health problems are produced, where systems can respond and what can be learned as action unfolds.

Foundational resource

Health worker training in community mobilisation

This manual provides a set of practical tools for Community Health Workers working in their designated areas as part of Ward-Based Primary Health Care Outreach Teams.

The tools support health workers to identify local priorities, analyse underlying causes, map stakeholders, plan collective responses and review what happens through implementation.

“The manual will go a long way in providing a practical and formal tool to guide Community Health Workers through their day-to-day work with communities.”Mr R Mabika · Acting sub-district PHC manager
