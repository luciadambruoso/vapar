Workshop: extending verbal autopsy

We organised two interactive workshops to evaluate the first complete action learning cycle of the VAPAR programme: one with senior managers in the provincial health department — Primary Health Care; Maternal, Child, Women, Youth Health and Nutrition; HIV/AIDS and Tuberculosis; and Community Based Services — held on 28 May 2019 at Ingwenyama, White River; and one with child health experts from the Ministerial Committee on Morbidity and Mortality in Children under 5 (CoMMiC) from four provinces, held on 30 May 2019 in Johannesburg.

The workshops presented the programme and data from the pilot and first cycle, drawing routine mortality from the MRC/Wits Agincourt HDSS with novel additions examining circumstances of mortality, developed in response to evidence gaps articulated by the Mpumalanga Department of Health. Across provincial and national stakeholders, VAPAR was regarded as a novel and relevant approach to facilitate community engagement and participation in health systems.

Recommendations included: involving traditional leaders and faith-based organisations as established community structures; including clinic operational managers and CHWs at all stages of cycle 2 with a focus on skills exchange; involving local municipal managers during the analyse, plan and act stages; convening stakeholders at the end of each cycle for collective reflection; VAPAR participation in routine district and sub-district planning and reporting; alignment with existing health structures at household/community and sub-district levels; a focus on community participation and contemporary priorities such as adolescent and mental health; refinement of the circumstances-of-mortality construct; and continued engagement with CoMMiC.
