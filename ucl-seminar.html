Improving data, improving health: verbal autopsy for health systems strengthening

Seminar at University College London, 17 October 2016, with participants from the WHO Information, Evidence and Research Unit, the Umeå WHO Collaborating Centre for VA, the Centre for Global Development at Aberdeen, the Malaria Consortium, the Africa Health Research Institute, UCL Institute for Global Health and Lancet Global Health. The seminar reflected on innovations in verbal autopsy — automated and mobile VA — and contextualised approaches to understanding why people die outside facilities or without registration. Programme (PDF)

Speakers and presentations: Dr Ties Boerma (WHO) on monitoring mortality and causes of death in the SDG era (PDF); Professor Peter Byass on pragmatic verbal autopsy for better health (PDF); Dr Edward Fottrell (UCL) on mobile VA, technological advances and ethical priorities; Dr Karin Källander (Malaria Consortium) on social autopsy as a method to examine barriers to care (PDF); Dr Lucia D’Ambruoso on VAPAR: people-centred, action-oriented method in South Africa (PDF); and Dr Kobus Herbst (Africa Health Research Institute) on interfacing HDSS, health services and international data communities (PDF).
