News · November 2016

Whose voices count? Doing research on the health of the poor

Part of the ESRC Festival of Social Science, 7 November 2016. How should we be doing research with people living in poverty, who are hard to reach, vulnerable, disadvantaged and socially excluded? This seminar brought together researchers working in different world regions to explore ‘people-centred’ and ‘action-oriented’ research on social and health inequalities, and launched the Centre for Global Development at the University of Aberdeen.

Making people count: the unequal world of global health data

Professor Peter Byass, Director, Umeå Centre for Global Health Research. An individual’s likelihood of having their birth and death officially registered still varies enormously around the world — with profound implications for health planning and policy. Presentation (PDF)

Working with people: a people-centred, action-oriented research project in South Africa

Dr Lucia D’Ambruoso, Deputy Director, Centre for Global Development. Recent estimates suggest two-thirds of the world’s deaths pass unrecorded, seriously limiting the ability of health systems to respond to the needs of vulnerable and excluded populations. The talk reported on a project employing a bottom-up philosophy connecting with population data at source. Presentation (PDF)
