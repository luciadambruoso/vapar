Provincial scale-up 2024–26 · Enhancing CHW roles · Strengthening rural PHC

Tools in use.

Community Health Workers play a vital role in bridging the gap between communities and the health system, particularly in rural primary health care. Across 2024, the VAPAR community mobilization training was delivered across Mpumalanga’s three health districts to 57 CHWs and Outreach Team Leaders — who then taught what they had learned to at least 280 others, on their own initiative. Two briefs set out what happened, and the route from project to system.

Supported by the UK Medical Research Foundation · Changing Policy and Practice Award · 2024–26

57CHWs and OTLs trained directly
3health districts: Ehlanzeni, Nkangala and Gert Sibande
21/22interviewees passed the training on to others
≥280additional people reached through peer sharing

Self-reported minimum additional reach; training fidelity was not formally assessed. The evaluation comprised project documentation and 22 interviews with CHWs and OTLs across 22 clinics.

From the closing remarks · provincial workshop · 25 March 2025

“We are pleased to confirm that the roles of Community Health Workers (CHWs) in Primary Health Care (PHC) have indeed been significantly enhanced through this initiative.” “We must work together to unblock the barriers that prevent the scaling up of this model, including the lack of integration into routine health system structures.” Dr Cheryl Nelson · Chief Director: Primary Health Care, Mpumalanga Department of Health

The evidence

How to read these findings

The evidence supports claims of feasibility, acceptability, reported capability-building, peer diffusion and early institutional uptake. It supports confidence in the intervention as a practical and transferable approach. It does not claim formal measurement of fidelity, long-term behavior change or patient-level outcomes.

The tools

Value in sequence

Each tool is simple on its own. Used in order, they carry a team from a recurring problem to shared analysis, clear roles, a workable plan and a record of what to pass on — then the cycle begins again.
